Case Study  ·  ChristianaCare Health System

SaaS Logistics Platform Implementation — Hospital Care at Home

Vendor
MedArrive
Stakeholder Scope
4–5 Groups
Timeline
MVP in 90–120 Days
Outcome
On-time Go-live + Expanded Paramedic Utilization
Problem

Real-time visibility — and a 90-day clock with no existing playbook.

ChristianaCare's Hospital Care at Home program needed a way to give virtual schedulers real-time visibility into paramedic location and availability across the service area. Without it, scheduling was based on estimated windows and manual check-ins, making it difficult to optimize in-home visit routing or respond dynamically to capacity gaps during the day.

Leadership set an aggressive MVP target of 90 to 120 days for a novel logistics platform that had not been deployed in this clinical context before. The organization had no existing playbook for this type of implementation, and there was no dedicated implementation team. I was designated as the sole technical lead representing ChristianaCare.

Approach

Full ownership, every dimension, simultaneously.

With full ownership of the implementation from kickoff through go-live, I managed every dimension of the project simultaneously:

  • Served as the sole point of contact between ChristianaCare and the MedArrive vendor team, owning all communication, escalation, and coordination across the engagement
  • Onboarded and aligned with the vendor's original product manager, then absorbed the transition when that PM departed 30 days into the engagement — rapidly bringing the incoming PM up to speed on agreed scope, progress, and outstanding decisions
  • Conducted UAT across platform features, documented defects and enhancement requests, and communicated findings back to the vendor in a structured and actionable format
  • Partnered with internal IT to manage access control, device provisioning, compliance requirements, and all required technical documentation for the deployment
  • Coordinated across 4–5 internal stakeholder groups including virtual schedulers, paramedics, frontline managers, and executive and corporate directors to align on readiness, workflows, and go-live criteria
  • Designed and delivered end-user training for 6 paramedics and 4 virtual schedulers, ensuring both groups could operate the platform independently from day one
  • Captured, prioritized, and communicated all post-launch feedback and issues back to the vendor, maintaining a clear feedback loop throughout the pilot and early adoption phases
Outcome

On time. And an insight that expanded capacity without adding headcount.

The platform went live on time, within the 90–120 day MVP window. Virtual schedulers gained real-time visibility into paramedic status and location across the service area, replacing manual coordination with a live operational picture.

Beyond the core scheduling capability, the implementation surfaced an unexpected and high-value operational insight: paramedics had significantly more available capacity than prior reporting had indicated. Visit windows estimated at 2–4 hours were consistently completing closer to 1–2 hours, revealing that paramedics could realistically absorb additional tasks between visits. This finding directly informed capacity planning and opened the door to expanding utilization without increasing headcount.

The platform also enabled visit check-in and check-out functionality visible to the MedArrive headquarters team, improving accountability and data fidelity at the organizational level.

Relevance

This engagement demonstrates end-to-end implementation ownership under compressed timelines, vendor relationship management through organizational instability, multi-level stakeholder coordination, UAT execution, and the ability to surface operational insights from newly deployed technology. It is representative of the dual BA and SA function performed across ChristianaCare's digital health portfolio.
